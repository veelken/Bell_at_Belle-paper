%steerability

A state that exhibits Bell non-locality, violating the inequality in \eq{eq:gen_Bell}, must also exhibit \textit{quantum steering}~\cite{schrodinger_1935,PhysRevLett.98.140402}: another effect of quantum correlations by which measurements performed on one of the entangled subsystems steer the quantum state of the other. A necessary and sufficient condition for the system of interest to exhibit steerability $S$ is~\cite{Afik:2022dgh}  
\be
S\qty[\CC]=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int \dd{\Omega} \sqrt{\hv \CC^T\CC\hv}>1
\ee
with $\hv$ being a unit vector varying with the solid angle $\Omega$. As we can see in Fig.~\ref{fig:m12}, the behaviour with the scattering angle is similar to that of $\Rchsh\qty[\CC]$.
